Our Mission
ACS inspires individuals
to become self-motivated learners and compassionate citizens who demonstrate respect, act with integrity, and seek intellectual growth in a culture of high expectations.
Our Value Proposition
Our diverse and compassionate community supports learners
to explore challenges and navigate perspectives through a comprehensive US curriculum while creating local and global connections.
Strategic Location
Our strategic location in the heart of the Middle East
opens up a world of opportunities. The historically rich landscapes of Jordan, including the wonders of Petra and the mystique of the Dead Sea, serve as the backdrop for our students' educational adventure. The experiences here are unlike any other, allowing them to live and learn in a place where history and tradition are intertwined.
Staff

the behind-the-scenes work of school staff is essential to create a conducive learning environment and ensure the smooth functioning of our school. Our staff creates a welcoming environment for both students and colleagues. Their dedication and hard work serve as the glue that holds the team together, fostering a sense of community and collaboration that enhances the learning experience for all.
Curriculum & Pedagogy
ACS offers an American curriculum that sparks
curiosity and ignites a lifelong love of learning. Our curriculum is firmly rooted in U.S. standards, ensuring our students receive an exceptional education that sets them up for future success. These standards include Common Core Math, Common Core Language Arts, Next Generation Science Standards (NGSS), National Core Arts Standards (NCAS), The Society of Health and Physical Educators (SHAPE) standards, The American Council on the Teaching of Foreign Languages (ACTFL) standards. ACS also uses the College, Career, and Civic Life (C3 Framework) for our Social Studies courses.

American Community School in Amman

The American Community School is a private, preparatory, international school in Amman, the capital of Jordan. It is an independent, coeducational day school which offers an American educational program from preschool (3 years old) through grade 12 for students of all nationalities. The School was founded in 1955.
The curriculum is that of U.S. schools. Instruction is in English. Arabic, Spanish, and French are all taught as foreign languages. There is no religious instruction. Advanced Placement courses are offered in 15 different courses. The School is accredited by the Middle States Association of Colleges and Schools.

Facilities & Faculty


The School is housed on approximately 9 acres (3.6 ha) of land in West Amman. Facilities include 60 classrooms, 5 modern science laboratories, a Collaborative Learning Commons which includes a maker space, one video and audio production studio, a sports and fine arts center, 2 playing fields, 2 tennis courts, and facilities for art, drama, and music education.
The faculty is composed of 79 members, including 48 U.S. citizens, 15 host-country nationals, and 16 third-country nationals. Over 75% of the teachers hold a master's degree in education.

Sports & Activities

Academic Games, Badminton, Band & Choir, Basketball, Forensics, Soccer, Swimming, Tennis, Track & Field, and Volleyball are offered to students at the Varsity and Junior Varsity levels. Junior Varsity (JV) is defined as students under the age of 16 as of September 1, and Varsity is defined as students under the age of 19 as of September 1 of that current year. At ACS, only students in 10th grade or higher are allowed to participate in Varsity sports. Only students in 8th grade or higher are allowed to participate in JV sports. Other groups and activities offered to students include Adventure Club, Arabic Club, Drama, National Honor Society, Model United Nations, and Student Council.
